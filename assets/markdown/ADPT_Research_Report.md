# ADPT — Adaptive Biotechnologies Research Report — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: 3-Step 10x Stock Checklist

All 3 conditions must be present simultaneously for a high-conviction 10x candidate.

---

## Step 1 — Sideways Base (Price Pattern)
- Stock must be in a multi-year sideways consolidation (12–36 months of tight, range-bound price action)
- The longer the base, the more powerful the eventual breakout ("the bigger the base, the higher in space")
- Rule: Do not buy a stock that has already broken out significantly from its base — the opportunity window is during or just at the breakout point
- Ideal entry: within 5% of the top of the multi-year range, confirmed with volume

## Step 2 — Growing Business (Fundamental Momentum)
- Revenue must be growing, not flat or declining, throughout the base period
- Earnings trajectory improving (moving from losses toward profitability, or expanding margins)
- Addressable market expanding (the problem the company solves is getting bigger)
- Rule: A sideways stock price + growing fundamentals = institutional accumulation is occurring (they're buying quietly — the "spring is coiling")

## Step 3 — Catalyst (The Trigger)
- A near-term visible catalyst that can force institutional re-rating:
  - FDA approval / clinical trial data
  - Major partnership or licensing deal
  - Index inclusion (S&P 500, Russell)
  - Transition to profitability (first positive EPS quarter)
  - Government contract win
- Rule: Do not wait for the catalyst to trigger — buy BEFORE the event if Steps 1 and 2 are confirmed
- If catalyst fails or is delayed, the base may reset; reassess rather than hold blindly

---

## The Spring Effect Pattern
- Definition: A stock coiling in a tight multi-year base while fundamentals improve, then releasing sharply upward when a catalyst hits
- The "spring" (compressed potential energy) is visible as: low volatility, narrowing trading range, declining volume during base
- Rule: The narrower the range in the final weeks before breakout, the more explosive the move typically is

---

## S&P 500 Concentration Warning (Risk Context)
- When S&P 500 top 10 stocks represent > 30% of total index weight, index returns become dependent on a handful of mega-caps
- This creates opportunity: smaller, under-owned stocks (like ADPT-type setups) are mispriced because passive money ignores them
- Rule: In high-concentration index environments, increase allocation to small/mid-cap 10x setups relative to index funds

---

## Entry/Exit Rules for 10x Setups
| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| All 3 steps confirmed, at base top | Enter; position size 3–7% of portfolio |
| Catalyst hits; price +20% in 1–2 weeks | Consider trimming 25–33% to reduce risk |
| Stock closes back inside the base (below breakout point) | Exit; thesis failed |
| Catalyst delayed but fundamentals intact | Hold; reset target timeline |
| Fundamental deterioration (revenue decline, cash crisis) | Exit immediately regardless of price |

---

## Portfolio Role
- 10x setups are high-conviction, higher-risk positions
- Limit total exposure to this style to 20–30% of portfolio
- Diversify across 3–5 such setups simultaneously (not all catalysts will trigger on schedule)
