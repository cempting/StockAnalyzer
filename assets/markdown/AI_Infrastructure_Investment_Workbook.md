# AI Infrastructure Investment Workbook — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: Picks-and-Shovels Strategy

Invest in the enablers of AI, not the applications. Infrastructure wins regardless of which AI model or application dominates.

---

## The Picks-and-Shovels Principle
- During the Gold Rush, shovel sellers made more consistent money than gold miners
- During Dot-Com, Cisco (networking) outperformed most internet application companies
- Rule: In any technology wave, weight infrastructure over applications (at least 50% of theme allocation)

---

## 4-Tier AI Infrastructure Stack

### Tier 1 — Semiconductors & Compute
- GPU manufacturers (Nvidia), chip designers (AMD, Broadcom), memory (Micron)
- Rule: Tier 1 is the highest-conviction long-term hold; entry on any 20%+ pullback from 52-week high

### Tier 2 — Data Center & Power
- Hyperscaler cloud providers (Microsoft Azure, AWS, Google Cloud)
- Data center REITs (Equinix, Digital Realty)
- Power infrastructure (utilities, nuclear, grid companies)
- Rule: Tier 2 benefits from multi-year capex cycle; buy and hold with 200-day MA floor

### Tier 3 — Networking & Cooling
- High-speed networking (Arista Networks, Marvell)
- Cooling/thermal management (Vertiv)
- Rule: Tier 3 is a secondary pick; enter only after Tier 1 and 2 positions are established

### Tier 4 — Supporting Services
- Testing, security, compliance for AI systems
- Rule: Tier 4 has the most speculative risk; limit to 5–10% of AI allocation; apply full 3-Filter test

---

## Historical Precedent Rules
- Dot-Com analogy: Infrastructure (Cisco, Intel) recovered after the bust; applications (Pets.com) went to zero
- Gold Rush analogy: Levi Strauss (jeans) and Wells Fargo (banking) outperformed most miners
- Rule: If you are unsure which AI application will win, own the infrastructure they all depend on

---

## Institutional Selling Rulebook
Watch for institutional distribution signals in AI infrastructure stocks:

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| Down days on 2x+ average volume | Reduce position by 25–50% |
| 3 consecutive weekly closes below 50-day MA | Exit remaining position |
| Institutional ownership declining ≥ 2 quarters | Reassess thesis; consider exit |
| Insider selling > 10% of shares in 90 days | Exit |
| Revenue growth deceleration ≥ 3 consecutive quarters | Exit |

---

## Capex Cycle Monitoring Rule
- Track quarterly capex announcements from Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta (the "hyperscalers")
- Rising hyperscaler capex = infrastructure demand rising = bullish for Tier 1–3
- Declining or paused capex = leading indicator of infrastructure slowdown; reduce exposure

---

## Position Sizing Rules for AI Infrastructure
- Tier 1: up to 15–20% of total portfolio
- Tier 2: up to 10–15% total (spread across 2–3 names)
- Tier 3: up to 5–10% total
- Tier 4: max 5% total (accept possible total loss)
- Total AI Infrastructure allocation: cap at 40–50% of portfolio to avoid theme concentration
