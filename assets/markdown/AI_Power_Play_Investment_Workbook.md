# AI Power Play Investment Workbook — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: 3-Layer AI Investment Stack

Invest across all 3 layers for balanced AI exposure. Weight based on risk tolerance.

---

## Layer 1 — Infrastructure (Lowest Risk, Foundational)
- Companies providing compute, chips, data centers, and power for AI
- Examples: Nvidia (GPUs), TSMC (chip fab), data center REITs, power/grid infrastructure
- Rule: This layer is the "picks-and-shovels" play — benefits regardless of which AI application wins

## Layer 2 — Platform / Model Providers (Medium Risk)
- Companies building and operating AI foundation models
- Examples: OpenAI (private), Anthropic (private), Google DeepMind, Meta AI
- Rule: Invest via public parent companies (Alphabet, Meta) rather than direct model providers (mostly private)

## Layer 3 — Application Layer (Highest Risk, Highest Upside)
- Companies embedding AI into vertical workflows (healthcare, legal, finance, coding)
- Rule: Apply full 3-Filter test (cash runway + institutional tailwind + revenue inflection) before buying any application-layer AI stock — many will fail

---

## 4-Type Risk Assessment
Before buying any AI stock, classify it:

| Type | Description | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| A — Infrastructure Core | Chip makers, data centers | Buy and hold; low volatility |
| B — Platform Enablers | Cloud AI APIs, model platforms | Hold with 150-day MA stop |
| C — Vertical AI Apps | Domain-specific AI software | Use 3-Power-Moves filter; small position |
| D — Speculative/Pre-revenue | AI startups, unproven models | Max 2–3% of portfolio; accept total loss risk |

---

## 3 Portfolio Templates

### Conservative (70% Layer 1, 20% Layer 2, 10% Layer 3)
- High infrastructure exposure; minimal speculative risk
- Suitable for 5+ year hold, low-volatility preference

### Balanced (40% Layer 1, 35% Layer 2, 25% Layer 3)
- Mix of stability and upside; requires quarterly rebalancing
- Rebalance when any layer drifts > 10% from target

### Aggressive (20% Layer 1, 30% Layer 2, 50% Layer 3)
- High upside potential; high volatility and drawdown risk
- Only suitable if max drawdown of 50%+ is acceptable

---

## Universal AI Investment Rules
- Do not concentrate > 30% in any single AI company (sector risk is correlated)
- AI theme can re-rate fast — use technical stops (150-day MA) even on "long-term" AI positions
- Avoid companies with vague "AI strategy" as the primary business without revenue evidence
- Monitor GPU supply chain: Nvidia revenue growth = leading indicator for entire AI stack
- Exit any Layer 3 position if it fails revenue inflection filter (see 3-Filter Framework)
