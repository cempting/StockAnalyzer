# Beginner Investor Workbook — Rule Compendium

## 40 Core Lessons Distilled into Rules

---

## Mindset Rules
1. Investing is long-term (5+ years); trading is short-term — know which you are doing
2. Never invest money you cannot afford to lose or will need within 2 years
3. Volatility is normal; drawdowns of 20–30% occur in most bull markets — do not panic-sell
4. Emotions (fear and greed) are the primary cause of retail underperformance — follow rules, not feelings
5. Your edge as a retail investor: time horizon and patience (institutions cannot hold positions for 10+ years)

---

## Money Basics Rules

### The 4% Rule
- In retirement, you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio per year without depleting it (assuming ~7% annual real returns)
- Rule: To retire on $50,000/year, you need $1,250,000 invested ($50,000 ÷ 4%)
- Build toward your "4% number" as the explicit savings goal

### Compounding
- Rule: Start investing as early as possible — time is the primary driver of compounding
- Doubling time ≈ 72 / annual return rate (e.g., at 9% → doubles every ~8 years)
- Do not interrupt compounding by withdrawing; let dividends and gains reinvest

### Emergency Fund First
- Rule: Maintain 3–6 months of expenses in cash before investing in equities
- Never invest emergency funds

---

## Smart Picks Rules
- Invest in businesses you understand (products, revenue model, competitive moat)
- Avoid complex financial products (leveraged ETFs, options, structured products) until experienced
- Diversify across at least 8–12 stocks and/or 2–3 sectors to reduce single-name risk
- Favor companies with growing revenue, positive free cash flow, and manageable debt

---

## Technical Tools Rules
- Use the 50-day and 200-day moving averages as trend filters:
  - Price above 200-day MA = bullish trend; price below = bearish
  - 50-day above 200-day = golden cross (buy signal); 50-day below = death cross (caution)
- Volume matters: a price breakout on high volume is more reliable than on low volume
- Don't buy a stock in a downtrend hoping for a reversal — wait for the trend to turn

---

## Habit Rules
- Review your portfolio monthly, not daily (daily checking increases emotional decisions)
- Document the thesis for every position: "I own this because…"
- If the thesis breaks, sell — do not hold hoping to recover
- Reinvest all dividends automatically
- Increase contribution amount by at least the rate of your salary increases each year

---

## Risk Management Rules
- Max single position: 10% of portfolio (for diversified investors)
- If a position drops > 20–25% and the thesis is unclear, reduce to half or exit
- Never use margin (borrowed money) as a beginner
- Set annual target return expectations conservatively (7–10% is excellent long-term)
