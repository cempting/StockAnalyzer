# Following Institutional Money Flow — Rule Compendium

## Core Argument
Retail investors cannot beat institutions on information speed. The edge is to identify where institutions are accumulating and follow them — not lead them.

---

## 2-Step Framework

### Step 1 — Identify the Hot Sector (Macro-First)
Use sector rotation signals to find where institutional capital is flowing:
1. Check which sector ETFs have the strongest 13-week and 26-week relative strength vs. S&P 500
2. Confirm with net fund flows (ETF inflows tracked via weekly flow data)
3. Confirm with earnings revision trends in that sector
- Rule: Only proceed to Step 2 in sectors showing all 3 signals positive

### Step 2 — Find the Best Stock in That Sector (3 Power Moves)
Within the confirmed hot sector, screen stocks for:
1. **Institutional volume spike** — single day volume ≥ 3x average on a strong up move
2. **Heartbeat pattern** — alternating high up-volume / low down-volume days in a base
3. **Record quarter** — best-ever revenue, earnings, or bookings reported in the past 8 weeks

- Rule: Stock needs ≥ 2 of 3 Power Moves to qualify for entry

---

## 8-Step Implementation Checklist
1. Run sector relative strength scan (weekly)
2. Confirm positive net flows into leading sector ETF
3. Check earnings revision direction for that sector
4. Screen stocks within sector for institutional volume spike (past 30 days)
5. Identify stocks in heartbeat base patterns
6. Filter for stocks with recent record quarters
7. Rank by combination of Power Moves (3 > 2 > 1)
8. Enter top-ranked stocks with pre-defined position size and stop-loss

---

## Institutional Tracking Rules
- Use 13F filings (quarterly) to verify institutional ownership increases
- Look for ≥ 2 different institutions raising stakes simultaneously
- Avoid stocks where institutional ownership is declining or insider selling is elevated

---

## Exit Rules
- Exit if the sector loses relative strength (drops out of top 3 sectors)
- Exit if any stock's individual technical setup breaks (close below 150-day MA)
- Exit if a record quarter is followed by a down-revision to guidance

---

## Key Principle
Sector first, stock second. Being in the right sector at the right time matters more than picking the "best" stock.
