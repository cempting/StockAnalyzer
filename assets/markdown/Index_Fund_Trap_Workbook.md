# Index Fund Trap Workbook — Rule Compendium

## Core Argument
Broad index funds (e.g., S&P 500) concentrate risk in overvalued mega-caps and provide zero sector alpha. Active sector rotation using ETFs outperforms in trending markets.

---

## 3-Step Sector Rotation Protocol

### Step 1 — Build the Scorecard
Score each major sector ETF weekly on 3 criteria (1–3 points each, max 9):
1. **Price relative strength** vs. S&P 500 (13-week and 26-week)
2. **Net fund flows** into sector ETF (positive = accumulation)
3. **Earnings revision trend** (analysts raising estimates = bullish)

- Score 7–9: BUY zone
- Score 5–6: HOLD / watch
- Score 1–4: AVOID / sell

### Step 2 — Buy the Sector Basket ETF
- When a sector scores ≥ 7, buy the corresponding sector ETF (not individual stocks)
- Examples: XLK (Technology), XLE (Energy), XLF (Financials), XLV (Healthcare), XBI (Biotech)
- Allocate equally across 2–3 top-scoring sectors; max 30–40% per sector

### Step 3 — Exit Trigger
- **Primary exit**: Price of sector ETF closes below its 150-day moving average
- **Secondary exit**: Scorecard drops to ≤ 4
- Do NOT hold through a 150-day MA break hoping for recovery; exit is non-negotiable

---

## The Index Trap Rules
- Avoid index funds when the top 5–10 stocks represent > 30% of the index weight
- Concentration risk: if mega-caps correct, the index falls regardless of other holdings
- Rule: During high-concentration periods, replace index ETF with equal-weight alternatives (e.g., RSP instead of SPY) or use sector rotation

---

## Comparison Framework
| | Index Fund | Sector Rotation |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | Automatic | Score ≥ 7 |
| Exit | Never (passive) | 150-day MA break or score ≤ 4 |
| Concentration | High | Controlled |
| Outperformance | Market = market | Possible if rotation is correct |

---

## Rules Summary
1. Score all sectors weekly; only hold sectors scoring ≥ 7
2. Use ETFs for sector exposure, not individual stocks (reduces single-name risk)
3. Exit any sector ETF below its 150-day MA — no exceptions
4. Never hold an overconcentrated index fund as a "safe" default during bull markets in narrow leadership
