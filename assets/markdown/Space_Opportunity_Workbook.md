# Space Opportunity Workbook — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: 3 Power Moves

A stock qualifies for entry when it demonstrates at least 2 of 3 Power Moves in recent price/volume history.

---

## Power Move 1 — Institutional Volume Spike
- Definition: A single trading day with volume ≥ 3x the 50-day average, accompanied by a price gain ≥ 3%
- Signals institutional (not retail) accumulation — funds cannot buy quietly at this scale
- Rule: One confirmed institutional volume spike in the past 30 days is required for highest-conviction entries

---

## Power Move 2 — Heartbeat Pattern
- Definition: Repeated alternating high-volume up days and low-volume down days within a consolidation
- The pattern shows buyers absorbing sellers without panic — controlled accumulation
- Rule: Look for ≥ 3 alternating cycles within a 4–6 week base before entry
- Invalid if down-volume days consistently exceed up-volume days

---

## Power Move 3 — Record Quarter
- Definition: Company reports its highest-ever quarter for revenue, earnings, or bookings
- A "record quarter" in any key metric removes doubt about business trajectory
- Rule: Entry is valid only within 4–8 weeks after the record quarter announcement (before re-rating is complete)

---

## IPO Trap Rule
- Do NOT buy a space/emerging-tech IPO in the first 6 months of trading
- Lock-up expiry (typically 90–180 days post-IPO) creates predictable selling pressure
- Rule: Wait until lock-up has expired AND stock has formed a proper base (≥ 6 weeks) before considering entry

---

## Index Rebalancing Effect
- Stocks added to major indices (S&P 500, Russell 2000, Nasdaq 100) face forced buying from passive funds
- Rule: When a space/emerging-tech stock confirms index inclusion, it qualifies as a short-term catalyst buy
- Entry: On the day of inclusion announcement; target 5–15% gain before forced buying is complete
- Exit: Take profit within 2–4 weeks of inclusion date (forced buying exhausts itself)

---

## General Rules
- Only apply this framework in sectors with confirmed institutional rotation (see Market Collision scorecards)
- Disqualify any stock where insider selling > insider buying in trailing 90 days
- Maximum position size for emerging/speculative sector plays: 5–10% of portfolio
