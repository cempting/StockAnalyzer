# The Last Easy Wealth Opportunities in 2026 — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: 3-Filter Stock Selection

Apply all 3 filters sequentially. A stock must pass all 3 to qualify.

---

## Filter 1 — Cash Runway
- Company must have ≥ 12 months of operating cash on hand at current burn rate
- Preferably ≥ 18–24 months (eliminates dilution risk from emergency capital raises)
- Rule: If cash runway < 12 months AND company is not profitable, skip the stock

### How to check
- Look at quarterly cash & equivalents on balance sheet
- Divide by quarterly operating cash burn (from cash flow statement)
- = months of runway

---

## Filter 2 — Institutional Tailwind
- At least one institutional investor (fund, ETF, or index) must be actively accumulating
- Evidence: rising institutional ownership % in recent 13F filings
- Rule: Look for ≥ 2 institutions increasing stake in the same quarter

### Red flag
- Insider selling + institutional selling in same quarter = disqualify regardless of fundamentals

---

## Filter 3 — Revenue Inflection
- Company must show a measurable acceleration in revenue growth, not just positive growth
- Evidence: Revenue growth rate increasing QoQ (e.g., +15% → +22% → +31%)
- Rule: Declining or decelerating revenue growth disqualifies a stock even if absolute growth is positive

### Bonus signal
- Gross margin expansion alongside revenue acceleration = strong confirmation of pricing power and scalability

---

## Portfolio Application Rules
- Build a basket of 4–8 stocks passing all 3 filters
- Weight equally or overweight highest-conviction names (max 25% per position)
- Reassess each position after every quarterly earnings report
- Exit any position where one filter fails (especially cash runway or revenue inflection)

---

## Context: 2026 Opportunity Theme
- Post-reset environment: companies that survived capital scarcity are now positioned to accelerate
- Focus on sectors with structural tailwinds: AI infrastructure, biotech catalysts, defense tech
- "Easy" window = period before these names get fully repriced by the market (likely 12–18 months)
