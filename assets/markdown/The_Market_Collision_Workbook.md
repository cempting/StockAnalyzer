# The Market Collision Workbook — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: Peace-to-Prosperity Pipeline

The macro causal chain determines which sectors to overweight. Follow the chain, not the news.

---

## The Causal Chain (read left to right)
Oil Prices ↓ → Interest Rates ↓ → Consumer Confidence ↑ → Spending ↑ → Corporate Earnings ↑ → Equity Markets ↑

### Monitoring Rules
- Track WTI crude oil price weekly; a sustained decline (>10% over 4–6 weeks) is a bullish macro signal
- Monitor 10-year Treasury yield; declining yield = tailwind for equities and growth stocks
- Use consumer confidence index (Conference Board) as a confirming signal — do not act on it alone

---

## 5 Capital Flow Waves (Sector Rotation Order)
When institutional money rotates, it follows this sequence:

1. **Defensive/Cash** (uncertainty/fear phase) — utilities, healthcare, bonds
2. **Financials** (rate environment improving) — banks, insurers
3. **Consumer Discretionary** (confidence returning) — retail, autos
4. **Technology/Growth** (expansion phase) — software, semiconductors
5. **Commodities/Energy** (late cycle, inflation pickup)

### Rule: Identify current wave position before buying individual stocks
- Do not buy growth/tech stocks if the market is still in Wave 1–2
- Buy leading stocks in the sector just beginning to attract capital inflows

---

## 6 Sector Scorecards
Rate each sector weekly on 3 factors (score 1–3 each, max 9):
1. Price relative strength vs. S&P 500
2. ETF fund flows (net inflows = positive)
3. Earnings revision trend (analysts raising or cutting estimates)

### Rules
- Only buy individual stocks in sectors scoring ≥ 7/9
- Sell or avoid sectors scoring ≤ 4/9
- Rebalance scorecard monthly

---

## Entry/Exit Rules
- **Enter**: Sector score ≥ 7 AND stock has base breakout with volume confirmation
- **Exit**: Sector score drops to ≤ 4 OR stock price closes below 150-day MA
- Never fight the macro chain: if oil is rising and rates are rising, reduce exposure to growth

---

## Key Insight
Individual stock selection is secondary. First identify the correct macro wave and sector. A mediocre stock in the right sector outperforms an excellent stock in the wrong sector.
