# Tom's DCA Strategy Guide — Rule Compendium

## Core Strategy: Dollar-Cost Averaging + Double-Down

---

## Standard DCA Rules
- Invest a fixed dollar amount at fixed intervals (e.g., weekly or monthly) regardless of price
- Never try to time the market; consistency is the edge
- Apply to high-conviction, long-term positions only (not speculative trades)

---

## Double-Down Trigger Rule
- If a stock drops ≥ 20% below its 52-week high, this triggers eligibility for a double-down purchase
- Double-down = buy an additional tranche equal to or larger than your standard DCA amount
- Rationale: Buying more of a fundamentally sound stock at a significant discount lowers average cost basis

### Double-Down Conditions (ALL must apply)
1. Stock is ≥ 20% below its 52-week high
2. The underlying business fundamentals are intact (no structural deterioration)
3. The drop is market/sector-driven, not company-specific bad news
4. You have available capital reserved for this purpose

---

## Risk Management Rules
- Predetermine maximum allocation to a single stock before initiating DCA
- Reserve at least 20–30% of intended position size as "double-down capital"
- Do NOT double down on stocks showing deteriorating earnings, revenue declines, or debt problems
- Set a hard floor: if stock drops > 50% from cost basis, re-evaluate thesis; do not automatically continue buying

---

## Psychological Rules
- Commit the DCA schedule in writing before starting
- Do not deviate based on short-term market sentiment or news noise
- Track average cost basis actively; your goal is to own more shares at a lower average cost over time

---

## When to Stop DCA
- Thesis is broken (fundamental deterioration confirmed)
- Position has reached maximum intended allocation
- Market enters confirmed primary downtrend (all major indices below 200-day MA)
