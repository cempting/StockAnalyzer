# Wall Street Protocol — Rule Compendium

## Core Framework: 4-Pillar Technical Analysis

All 4 entry conditions must be TRUE simultaneously. Any 1 exit condition triggers a sell.

---

## Entry Rules (ALL must be met)

### Pillar 1 — Trend Confirmation
- Price must be ABOVE the 150-day moving average
- 150-day MA must be trending UP (not flat or declining)
- 50-day MA must be ABOVE the 200-day MA (golden cross)

### Pillar 2 — Relative Strength
- Mansfield Relative Strength (RS) must be POSITIVE (above zero line)
- Stock must be outperforming the S&P 500 over the past 52 weeks
- RS line must be trending up at time of entry

### Pillar 3 — Price Base / Pattern
- Stock must be emerging from a multi-week consolidation base (min. 6–8 weeks)
- Base should be tight, low-volatility sideways action
- Price must be within 5–15% of 52-week high at time of entry

### Pillar 4 — Volume Confirmation
- Breakout from base must occur on volume ≥ 40–50% above 50-day average volume
- Institutional accumulation (large up-volume days > down-volume days) visible in base

---

## Exit Rules (ANY triggers a sell)

1. Price closes below the 150-day MA
2. 50-day MA crosses below 200-day MA (death cross)
3. Mansfield RS drops below zero
4. Stock gives back >7–8% from entry price (stop-loss)
5. Position loses >2x ATR (average true range) from entry

---

## Position Sizing Rules
- Max position size: 10–20% of portfolio per stock
- Never add to a losing position
- Scale in: start at 50% intended size; add remaining on confirmation

---

## General Principles
- Only trade in the direction of the primary market trend
- Avoid stocks in declining sectors regardless of individual setup
- Review all 4 pillars weekly; exit quickly if conditions deteriorate
- Keep a watchlist of 10–20 stocks meeting 3 of 4 pillars (pre-qualified candidates)
